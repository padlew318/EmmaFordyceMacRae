Emma Fordyce MacRae  populated her landscapes with figures but there was little movement and in this painting a timeless quality pervades the view.  MacRae avoided the open vista of the ocean preferring inlets, harbors and beaches and in Children on the Beach she has reinforced the organisation of her composition of the picnic by focusing on the granite rocks. The white ground is visible through the thinner layers of paint laid on top and  has become part of the composition. 

 

MacRae’s summer studio was above Stage Fort Park in Gloucester, MA and this Cape Ann scene is probably a nearby beach with the view of Gloucester in the background.
