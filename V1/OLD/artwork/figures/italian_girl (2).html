Emma Fordyce MacRae 20th Century American Artist

The theme of women in interiors has a long history and in the twentieth century was mostly associated with academic painters, especially the artists of the Boston School.

Emma Fordyce MacRae's friend Ivan Olinsky also treated this subject, but also with a more traditional approach. MacRae's paintings differ, however, in their style from either his work or those artists of the Boston School in that hers "suggest a simplification of form not unlike that practiced by many a modern sculptor." This treatment is seen, for example, in the planes of the face and neck of the sitter in this painting of Olinsky's daughter.
